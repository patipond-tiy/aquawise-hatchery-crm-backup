## The Audit Lenses (5 lenses)

| # | Lens | What it checks | Evidence rule |
|---|------|----------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Job fit (J1–J5)** | Story serves ≥1 of J1–J5 from `00-overview.md`. Name the served job(s). | Cite J# + a one-clause reason |
| 2 | **Phase fit** | Story's H-phase tag is correct given (a) the story's content and (b) its dependencies — a H1 story whose blockers are H2 fails this lens. | Name the served phase (e.g., `H1`, `H1→H2`); if violation, cite the blocker dependency |
| 3 | **Non-negotiable + ⚠ hypothesis** | Story respects each of the 7 non-negotiables. If the story touches a v0.5 ⚠ surface in `aquawise-updated-docs/06`, it must itself carry a ⚠ flag in its Dev Notes or Domain Framing section. | Cite `rule#X` + AC# or short quote |
| 4 | **Cross-doc consistency** | Persona reference matches `01-personas.md` (P1–P6 + day-in-life); auth role matches `08-roles-and-rls.md` (`owner`/`counter_staff`/`lab_tech`/`auditor`); RBAC matrix correct; referenced tables/columns exist in migrations; Today-state matches `02-feature-inventory.md`; references in story footer (`[Source: ...]`, `[Blocked-by: ...]`) resolve. | Cite the specific cross-doc anchor + the conflicting field in the story file |
| 5 | **Internal clarity** | Each AC is testable, atomic, and unambiguous; "As X, I want Y, so that Z" is present and complete; Tasks reference AC numbers; Dev Notes domain framing is concrete; no contradictions between AC and Tasks; no TBD or placeholder values. | Cite AC# + what makes it ambiguous |

### The 7 non-negotiables (numbered for cell citations)

From `docs/product-spec/00-overview.md` § "What we explicitly *don't* do":

1. **`rule#1`** — We do not sell to farmers. Farmer-facing surfaces are free, always.
2. **`rule#2`** — We do not charge per message, per cert, or per export. Flat hatchery subscription only.
3. **`rule#3`** — We do not white-label. The "Verified by AquaWise" stamp is the moat.
4. **`rule#4`** — We do not replace LINE for farmer chit-chat. We push branded Flex; H3 hosts persistent thread; we don't capture social conversation.
5. **`rule#5`** — We do not sell hardware, IoT, or feed.
6. **`rule#6`** — We do not chase volume hatcheries who optimize for PL/day at any quality. The buyer is the careful operator.
7. **`rule#7`** — We do not ship features without a hatchery customer asking for them. "Slow is right."

## Cell Conventions

For lenses 1, 3, 4, 5:

- `✓` — clean / aligned
- `⚠️ <reason>` — concern; cite the specific AC# or short quote from the story file
- `❌ <reason>` — violation; cite the specific AC# or short quote
- `neutral` — story is enabling/infra (e.g., a pure migration); lens doesn't directly apply

For lens 2 (Phase):

- Name the phase served (`H1`, `H2`, `H3`, or `H1→H2` for a story with H2 dependencies tagged H1).
- If the tagged phase ≠ the actually-correct phase, prefix with `❌ tagged-H1 but H2` (or similar) and cite the blocker.

**The `⚠️`-must-cite-evidence rule is the most important convention.** Without
it, you get vibes-based audits that nobody can verify. A worker who writes
`⚠️ unclear` without an AC# or quote has failed the audit.

## Action Verdicts

The final column carries exactly one of:

- **`KEEP`** — ship as-is. All five lenses ✓ or `neutral`.
- **`FIX-AUTO: <one-line direction>`** — keep the story; concern is mechanically
  fixable by a coding agent without product judgment. Examples:
  - Re-tag phase (H1 → H2 because blocker is H2)
  - Fix persona reference (P3 Rep → P2 Manager per `01-personas.md`)
  - Fix auth role string (admin/editor/viewer → owner/counter_staff/lab_tech)
  - Restructure an AC for testability ("works on mobile" → "renders at ≥360px viewport with no horizontal scroll")
  - Add a missing ⚠ flag in Dev Notes when the story touches a v0.5 ⚠ surface
  - Fix a broken `[Source: …]` reference path
  - Rename a referenced column to match what exists in migrations
- **`FIX-REVIEW: <one-line direction>`** — keep the story; concern needs human/
  product judgment before any code change. Examples:
  - Story implies pricing or business-policy change (touches `rule#2`)
  - Story depends on an unvalidated v0.5 hypothesis that needs P'Bunjong sign-off
  - Story scope contradicts a non-negotiable in a way that requires a product call
  - Cross-doc disagreement where the docs themselves diverge (need to decide which is canonical)
  - AC implies a UX choice that affects brand voice (`07-brand-and-voice.md`)
- **`CUT`** — drop from current scope. Misaligned with vision; non-negotiable
  violation that can't be reframed; doesn't serve any of J1–J5.
- **`DEFER to H2`** or **`DEFER to H3`** — premature; move to a later phase
  when prerequisites are met.

**Action precedence** (when multiple issues hit one story): `CUT` > `DEFER` >
`FIX-REVIEW` > `FIX-AUTO` > `KEEP`. Pick the most severe required action.

No `TBD`, no `review later`, no blanks.
