# AquaWise for Hatcheries — Customer-Based Brand Equity (CBBE) Model
## Applying Keller's Brand Pyramid to the Thai Shrimp Hatchery Market

**Author:** Chain (CEO) & founders
**Status:** Brand strategy document, April 2026
**Purpose:** Define what AquaWise stands for in the mind of the hatchery owner, layer by layer, so that every brand decision (visual, verbal, behavioral) can be tested against a coherent brand framework.
**Reads alongside:** Business Strategy Doc (value, JTBD, STP). This document is the brand-building counterpart.

---

## 1. Why CBBE for AquaWise

A startup at the seed stage is not normally where brand equity work happens. The instinct is "first build the product, then build the brand." That instinct is wrong for AquaWise specifically, for one reason: **AquaWise is a trust product.**

In a trust product, brand is not decoration applied after the product works. Brand *is* the product. The hatchery owner who decides whether to put a QR code on their counter is not evaluating features. They are evaluating whether AquaWise is the kind of thing they want their customers, their association peers, and their own self-image to be associated with. The brand decision and the product decision are the same decision.

This is why we build the brand pyramid now, before we have 20 hatcheries. The pyramid is not a marketing exercise. It is a prediction of the path AquaWise must walk if it is going to become the trusted infrastructure of Thai aquaculture in a 10-year timeframe.

We use Keller's CBBE model because it forces a discipline: brand equity is built in a specific order. You cannot have resonance without judgments. You cannot have judgments without performance and imagery. You cannot have any of it without salience. Skipping a layer is the most common reason startups have weak brands at scale. We will not skip layers.

## 3. Level 1 — Salience: "Who are you?"

Salience is the depth and breadth of brand awareness. It is the question: when a hatchery owner thinks about a problem AquaWise can solve, does AquaWise come to mind? When they encounter the AquaWise name, do they recognize it? When they think of the category we play in, are we one of the names they list?

For most startups, salience work is "build awareness." For AquaWise, salience work is more specific: we must own a precise category, in a precise way, in a precise community.

### 3.1 The category we are establishing

Hatchery owners do not currently have a category for what we do. There is no Thai phrase for "closed-loop trust system for shrimp hatcheries." This is both a problem (no existing demand category) and an opportunity (we get to define the category).

The category we are establishing in the hatchery owner's mind is:

> **"ระบบที่ติดตามกุ้งหลังขาย จากโรงเพาะถึงจับ"** ("the system that tracks shrimp after the sale, from hatchery to harvest")

This phrase does the work because it is concrete (not "platform" or "AI"), it is about the customer's job (tracking, not analyzing), and it has a beginning and an end (from sale to harvest, not "always").

If a hatchery owner can repeat this phrase to a peer, salience is strong. If they say "AquaWise is a tech company" or "AquaWise is an AI thing," salience is weak.

### 3.2 The needs we must be remembered for

When a hatchery owner has any of the following needs, AquaWise must be one of the first three names that comes to mind:

| The need they feel | The way they express it | What we want them to think |
|---|---|---|
| Customer slipping away | "ลูกค้าไม่กลับมา" | "I should check AquaWise — when is he restocking?" |
| Unfair blame | "เกษตรกรว่า PL ไม่ดี" | "What does AquaWise say about that batch?" |
| Quality verification | "พิสูจน์ว่าโรงเพาะเราดี" | "AquaWise scorecard." |
| Modern operating | "อยากเป็นโรงเพาะที่จริงจัง" | "Get into the AquaWise system." |
| Customer acquisition | "อยากได้ลูกค้าใหม่" | "Put up the AquaWise QR." |

Five needs. Five mental triggers. The brand-building work at the salience level is hammering these associations until they become reflexive.

### 3.3 Where salience lives in 2026

Salience is built in specific places. For a new entrant in a relationship-driven SME industry, broad-based marketing (Facebook ads, search ads, LinkedIn) is wasted effort. Salience lives in:

- **Association meetings.** P'Pong's monthly Thai Shrimp Larvae Hatchery Association sessions. Each AquaWise mention there is worth more than a thousand impressions on Facebook.
- **The hatchery counter itself.** The QR poster is salience infrastructure. Every farmer who scans it sees the AquaWise name in a moment of trust.
- **Farmer-to-hatchery conversations.** When a farmer asks "do you do batch certificates like that other hatchery?" we have salience.
- **Industry events.** Thai Aquaculture Federation events, Department of Fisheries roundtables, university aquaculture programs.
- **The crisis moment.** The moment a hatchery is being unfairly blamed for a crop failure and an association peer says "have you tried AquaWise?"

We will not buy display advertising in 2026. We will not run Google Ads. We will not promote on Facebook beyond organic content. Every brand budget baht goes to relationship infrastructure.

### 3.4 Salience health check (year-end 2026)

By December 2026, salience is healthy if:
- 100% of Chachoengsao Shrimp Larvae Hatchery Association members can correctly identify what AquaWise does in one sentence.
- 50% of mid-sized hatchery owners outside Chachoengsao have heard the AquaWise name from a peer (not from us).
- Every farmer in the AquaWise LINE OA can describe the brand to another farmer accurately.

## 4. Level 2a — Performance: "What are you (functionally)?"

Performance is the functional substance of the brand. It is what the product *actually does,* tested against the customer's real needs. Everything aspirational in this document collapses if performance is weak.

For AquaWise hatcheries, performance has five dimensions.

### 4.1 Primary characteristics and features

**The closed loop is the primary feature.** Every hatchery owner who explains AquaWise to another hatchery owner must be able to start with: *"It tracks PL from sale to harvest. Both sides. Hatchery and farmer."* If they cannot say this, performance positioning has failed.

Secondary features (in order of perceived importance):
1. The batch certificate sent to the farmer's LINE on the day of sale.
2. The Day-30 and Day-60 survival report.
3. The restock timing predictor.
4. The hatchery scorecard (Phase H3+).
5. The cross-farm same-batch comparison (Phase H3+).

The order matters. We do not lead with the scorecard, even though the scorecard is our most defensible long-term moat. We lead with the certificate because it is the most immediate, tangible benefit and the easiest to explain.

### 4.2 Product reliability, durability, serviceability

A trust product cannot afford to be flaky. Reliability is brand equity, not engineering metrics.

- **Data accuracy.** Every number we display is externally verifiable. A wrong survival number, a wrong batch ID, a wrong certificate field is a brand wound that takes months to heal. Our internal standard: **truth over speed.** A delayed certificate is forgivable; an inaccurate one is not.
- **System uptime.** The LINE bot must respond. The webapp must load. The certificate must generate. Downtime in front of a watching hatchery counter is a story they will tell their peers.
- **Durability of the data.** Hatcheries trust us with multi-year operational records. We treat their data as a 10-year commitment, not a 12-month feature. Data export is built in from Phase H1.
- **Serviceability.** When something breaks, the hatchery owner reaches a human, in Thai, within hours. Not a chatbot. Not a help center. A human. In the early phase that human is the founders themselves.

### 4.3 Service effectiveness, efficiency, empathy

The brand is shaped by service interactions as much as by product interactions. The hatchery owner who emails about a batch entry problem and gets a precise, calm, Thai-language reply within four hours has just learned something about AquaWise that no marketing can convey.

Service principles for the brand:
- **Calm under pressure.** A frustrated customer needs the AquaWise contact to feel slower and more confident than they are.
- **Direct, not deflective.** "I don't know yet, I will find out by 6pm" is brand-strengthening. "We are looking into it" is brand-weakening.
- **Empathy without theater.** The Thai service voice is warm but not effusive. Real, not performative.

### 4.4 Style and design

The aesthetic of AquaWise is a brand asset. The visual language is the brand's body language.

Style commitments:
- **Clean, calm, careful.** Not slick. Not minimalist-flexing. Just confident and unhurried.
- **The Logo Blue / Dark Cyan palette is the protagonist.** Color used for emphasis is precious; we spend it carefully.
- **A two-tier type system.** Inter + Noto Sans Thai + JetBrains Mono on product surfaces (CRM, dashboards, LIFF, data-bearing Flex Messages). Plus Jakarta Sans + Inter + Noto Sans Thai on brand surfaces (QR poster, batch certificate, marketing site, pitch decks). Inter does the precision work; Plus Jakarta Sans does the personality work; Noto Sans Thai pairs with both at matching x-heights for clean bilingual rendering. No other typefaces.
- **The certificate is the brand moment.** The first time a farmer receives a batch certificate is the first time they feel AquaWise as a serious thing. Every certificate must look authoritative. Not bureaucratic. Not slick. Authoritative.

Style anti-commitments (what we explicitly do not do):
- No motion graphics, animations, or splash screens.
- No glassmorphism, neumorphism, or whatever the fashionable style is in 2026.
- No emoji-heavy communication on professional surfaces.
- No "AI" iconography (no glowing brains, no neural network graphics).

### 4.5 Price as a performance signal

Price communicates positioning. Free communicates "you are the product." Premium communicates "you are exclusive." We sit in the middle.

The 2,000–5,000 baht/month flat hatchery subscription says three things simultaneously:
- This is real. It costs money.
- This is sustainable. It is not predatory.
- This is accessible. The President of an association does not have to defend the price.

Pricing is brand equity at the performance level because it shapes who the customer thinks the product is for. Free positions us as charity (low quality). 50,000 baht/month positions us as enterprise (irrelevant). The current pricing positions us as serious, professional, and accessible to the Tier 2 hatchery we are targeting.

## 5. Level 2b — Imagery: "What are you (abstractly)?"

If performance is what the brand does, imagery is what the brand *means.* It is the abstract associations the customer makes. For most startups, imagery work is the most underdeveloped layer. We invest in it deliberately.

### 5.1 User profiles — who AquaWise is for, in the customer's imagination

Hatchery owners do not just buy products; they buy the imagined community of users. The question is not "what features?" but "what kind of hatchery uses AquaWise?"

The user profile we want to project:

> *The AquaWise hatchery is the careful, modern, association-affiliated operator. Not the biggest. Not the loudest. The one that runs a clean PCR program, ships consistent batches, has been in business 15+ years, and has nothing to hide. The kind of hatchery whose owner shows up to association meetings on time and listens before speaking. The kind of hatchery the farmers ask about by name.*

Hatchery owners who see themselves in this profile will gravitate toward AquaWise. Hatchery owners who see themselves as "the hustler," "the volume player," or "the disruptor" will not, and that is fine — they are not our target.

### 5.2 Purchase situations — when AquaWise comes to mind

Imagery includes the specific circumstances under which the brand is recalled. We want AquaWise to be top-of-mind in these moments:

- **At an association meeting** when someone mentions customer churn.
- **At the counter** when a sale is being made and the farmer hesitates.
- **At 11pm** when the owner is mentally going through tomorrow's worry list.
- **In a customer dispute** when blame is being assigned.
- **In a peer conversation** when one hatchery owner asks another how they handle restock timing.

Brand storytelling, sales conversations, and content all work to embed AquaWise in these specific moments.

### 5.3 Brand personality and values

Brand personality is the human characteristics ascribed to the brand. If AquaWise were a person, who would they be?

| Trait | What it means | What it rules out |
|---|---|---|
| **ลูกหลานที่เรียนมา** | The educated younger relative who came back to help. Capable but humble. Knows things, but listens first. | Not the consultant from Bangkok. Not the foreign expert. Not the slick salesperson. |
| **Calm under pressure** | When everyone else is panicking, AquaWise is the steady voice. | Not breathless. Not urgent. Not crisis-driven. |
| **Direct, not flashy** | Says the truth in plain words. Does not use jargon to impress. | Not "innovative AI-powered." Not "revolutionary platform." |
| **Patient with people, impatient with noise** | Spends real time with hatchery owners, but cuts through hype quickly. | Not "always responsive." Not "low-touch SaaS." |
| **Anti-eFishery** | Slow, transparent, careful. The opposite of the unicorn that overpromised. | Not "scaling rapidly." Not "moving fast." |

The personality is consistent across every brand surface: the LINE bot voice, the email tone, the founder Twitter posts, the pitch deck language. If a piece of communication does not match the personality, it does not ship.

### 5.4 Brand values

Values are the operating principles the brand will not compromise on, even when it is convenient to do so.

1. **Truth over comfort.** Every number externally verifiable. No fabricated metrics. The anti-eFishery standard.
2. **Farmers free, always.** Farmers pay zero. This is not a marketing gimmick; it is a structural commitment.
3. **Neutrality is the moat.** No equity from feed companies, hatchery groups, or integrators. No exclusive deals. No conflicts of interest.
4. **Slow is right.** We will not expand faster than the data allows. We will not ship features the data does not support. Patience is a brand value, not a weakness.
5. **The hatchery owner is the protagonist.** AquaWise is the supporting character. Their craft is the story. We are the tool.

### 5.5 Heritage and history

Imagery includes the brand's origin story. AquaWise has a heritage even at this early stage, and we should articulate it deliberately because it will be told whether we shape it or not.

The heritage story we are building:

> *AquaWise began in 2025 when Chain, a Chulalongkorn computer engineering student, built VannameiVision — an AI system for grading shrimp post-larvae quality — and realized the harder problem was not the technology but the trust. He partnered with Dr. Chanati Jantrachotechatchawan (Harvard molecular biology, King's College PhD neuroscience, IBO 2006 gold medalist) and Dr. Kobchai Duangrattanalert (Manchester PhD computational genetics) to build a system that closed the loop between hatchery and farm. The first hatchery to come on board was P'Pong's, the President of the Thai Shrimp Larvae Hatchery Association in Chachoengsao. Everything since has been built on that foundation: one province, one association, one careful step at a time.*

This story is true. It is also strategically chosen. It emphasizes Thai roots, technical credentials, association endorsement, and patient growth. It does not emphasize venture capital, Silicon Valley training, or "AI revolution." Those things are true too, but they are not the story we lead with for hatcheries.

## 6. Level 3a — Judgments: "What about you (rationally)?"

Judgments are the customer's rational evaluations of the brand. After they know who we are (salience), what we do (performance), and what we mean (imagery), they form opinions. These opinions are testable and we should test them.

Keller divides judgments into four categories.

### 6.1 Brand quality

Quality judgments are formed by the customer's experience of performance and imagery. For AquaWise hatcheries, quality judgments are dominated by data accuracy.

The judgment we want: *"AquaWise's data is right. When they tell me my batch hit 78% Day-30 survival across 14 farms, I can take that to the bank."*

The judgment that would kill us: *"AquaWise's numbers are off. Last week the system said my customer was at Day 95 but the farmer told me he had already harvested."*

Every operational decision must be tested against this. A small discrepancy in a Day-30 number, surfaced to a hatchery owner, is more damaging than a missed feature deadline.

### 6.2 Brand credibility

Credibility has three sub-dimensions:

**Expertise.** Does the brand have the knowledge and capability to deliver?

We build expertise through:
- Founder credentials (Chain's TensorFlow certification + Chula CEDT, Dr. Chanati's Harvard / King's College / IBO gold, Dr. Kobchai's Manchester PhD).
- VannameiVision as a demonstration of technical capability (5-stage AI pipeline, 98.6–99.4% accuracy).
- Industry endorsements (P'Pong as President of the Hatchery Association, P'Bunjong at the Federation level).
- Public technical content (blog posts, conference talks, association presentations) that show the depth of thinking.

**Trustworthiness.** Does the brand do what it says, dependably and honestly?

We build trustworthiness through:
- Visible non-affiliation. We do not take feed company equity. We do not sign exclusive deals. We are visibly neutral.
- Consistency. The brand voice, behavior, and product experience are the same on day 1 and day 365.
- Anti-eFishery posture. Every move that contradicts an eFishery move builds trust.
- Public corrections. When we make a mistake, we say so publicly. Mistakes hidden become brand wounds; mistakes acknowledged become brand strength.

**Likability.** Does the customer enjoy interacting with the brand?

We build likability through:
- The ลูกหลาน persona of the bot voice. Warm without being syrupy.
- The founder's personal presence in early customer relationships. P'Pong does not just buy a product; he gets to know Chain, Dr. Chanati, Dr. Kobchai personally.
- Small acts of generosity. Free price feed. Free certificates for Phase H1. Free onboarding support.

### 6.3 Brand consideration

Consideration is whether the customer puts the brand on their mental shortlist when they have a need we serve. This is a higher bar than salience (which is just awareness).

The judgment we want: *"When I need to solve customer retention or batch verification, AquaWise is one of the two or three names I would seriously consider."*

The judgment that would worry us: *"I have heard of AquaWise but I would not actually try it."*

Consideration is built through evidence. The first hatchery success story (P'Pong's documented retention lift) is consideration infrastructure. The first defended-against-unfair-blame case study is consideration infrastructure. We should publish these as soon as they are real.

### 6.4 Brand superiority

Superiority is whether the customer thinks the brand is better than alternatives. This requires the customer to have alternatives in mind.

For AquaWise, superiority claims must be specific and defensible:

- *"AquaWise is the only product that closes the loop between PL sold and harvest delivered."* (Defensible.)
- *"AquaWise is the only product where farmers pay nothing."* (Defensible.)
- *"AquaWise is the only product without hardware in Thai aquaculture."* (Defensible.)
- *"AquaWise is the most accurate batch certification in Thailand."* (Not yet defensible. We should not say this until we have years of comparative data.)

The brand discipline is to make only the claims we can defend, every time. Overclaiming on superiority is the eFishery trap.

## 7. Level 3b — Feelings: "What about you (emotionally)?"

Feelings are the emotional responses the brand evokes. For a SaaS product, feelings work is unusual. For a trust product, feelings work is essential. The hatchery owner's relationship with AquaWise is not primarily transactional. It is emotional, even when the owner does not articulate it that way.

Keller identifies six brand feelings. We want AquaWise to evoke specific ones, and not others.

### 7.1 Primary feelings to cultivate

**Security.** This is the most important feeling AquaWise must evoke. The hatchery owner who uses AquaWise should feel: *"I am protected. Unfair blame cannot ruin me. I have a defense."*

Every product feature, every customer service interaction, every piece of brand communication should reinforce security. The certificate, the Day-30 report, the cross-farm comparison, the scorecard — these are not just features; they are security artifacts.

**Self-respect.** The second most important feeling. The hatchery owner who uses AquaWise should feel: *"My craft is recognized. The work I have been doing for 25 years is finally visible to the people who matter."*

This is why the scorecard, when it launches, must be presented as a recognition of quality, not a measurement of compliance. The hatchery is being seen for what they have always been; AquaWise is just the lens.

### 7.2 Secondary feelings (worth cultivating, less important)

**Warmth.** The bot voice, the founder's personal presence, the ลูกหลาน persona. AquaWise should feel like a younger relative who came back to help, not a vendor.

**Social approval.** The hatchery that uses AquaWise feels well-regarded by their association peers. This builds slowly as more association members adopt; we should not force this feeling early or it feels manufactured.

### 7.3 Feelings to avoid

Some feelings would actively damage the brand. We name them so we can monitor for them.

- **Excitement.** AquaWise is not exciting. AquaWise is reassuring. Excitement is the feeling associated with eFishery's pitch decks. We are the opposite.
- **Fun.** Aquaculture is serious work. The hatchery owner does not want a fun product. They want a careful one.
- **FOMO.** "Hurry, limited spots, exclusive access." These are scarcity tactics. They feel like sales pressure. We do not use them.
- **Anxiety.** A product that creates anxiety to sell solutions (like "your business is at risk") is manipulative. We do not. We meet hatchery owners where they already feel anxiety, and we offer relief, not amplification.

### 7.4 The feeling test

Before any major brand communication ships, it should pass the feeling test:

> *Does this make the hatchery owner feel more secure and more respected? If yes, it is on-brand. If it makes them feel excited, fearful, urgent, or impressed, it is off-brand.*

This is a high bar. It rules out most of what aspiring SaaS marketers are taught to do. That is the point.

## 8. Level 4 — Resonance: "What about you and me?"

Resonance is the top of the pyramid. It is the depth of the relationship between the customer and the brand. Most customers of most brands never get here. Resonance is where loyalty becomes identity.

Keller identifies four sub-dimensions of resonance. For AquaWise hatcheries, each one has a specific shape.

### 8.1 Behavioral loyalty

Behavioral loyalty is the repeat behavior the customer engages in. For AquaWise hatcheries:

- **Monthly subscription renewal** without question, year after year.
- **Daily batch entry workflow** as habitual as making coffee in the morning.
- **Annual usage growth** — the hatchery uses more of the product over time, not less.
- **Tolerance for occasional bugs.** A brand without resonance gets churned over a single bug; a brand with resonance gets a polite email and a renewed subscription.

Behavioral loyalty is the easiest dimension to measure. Renewal rate, daily active hatcheries, feature adoption — these are the metrics.

### 8.2 Attitudinal attachment

Attitudinal attachment is when the customer feels the brand is *part of who they are.* Not just a tool they use; an extension of their identity.

The signal we are looking for: a hatchery owner who, when asked to describe their business at an association meeting, says *"we are an AquaWise hatchery"* the way a farmer might say *"we are an organic farm."* The brand becomes part of the self-description.

We do not engineer this. It emerges, slowly, when the brand has done everything else right. But we can recognize it when it appears, and we should treat the hatchery owners who reach this state as the most precious customers we have. They are the foundation of everything that follows.

### 8.3 Sense of community

Resonance includes the customer's connection to other customers. Hatchery owners who use AquaWise should feel part of something larger than themselves.

We build this through:
- **AquaWise hatchery roundtables.** Quarterly gatherings of hatchery customers in Chachoengsao (in person or virtual). Best practices, problems, peer learning. The roundtable is the brand event.
- **Association integration.** AquaWise content and tools designed to be shared inside the Hatchery Association. The brand becomes part of the association's value, not separate from it.
- **Public scorecard tier.** When the scorecard launches, hatcheries inside the verified tier are publicly part of a community. Belonging to the tier is itself the community.

### 8.4 Active engagement

Active engagement is when the customer puts energy into the brand beyond what they pay for. They become advocates, contributors, even co-creators.

The signals:
- A hatchery owner unprompted recommends AquaWise to another hatchery owner.
- A hatchery owner contributes feature ideas that we implement.
- A hatchery owner speaks about AquaWise at an association meeting without being asked.
- A hatchery owner writes a public testimonial without being asked.

Active engagement is the rarest brand state, and the most valuable. The hatchery owner at this level is not a customer anymore. They are part of the team, in spirit if not in title.

## 9. Brand Architecture — AquaWise vs. AquaWise for Hatcheries

The CBBE pyramid above is for the hatchery channel specifically. But AquaWise is one brand serving multiple stakeholders: farmers, hatcheries, ล้ง brokers, feed companies, eventually banks. We need to articulate how the master brand and the channel-specific brand relate.

The answer is: **AquaWise is one brand with one personality, one set of values, and one visual identity. The product surfaces vary by stakeholder, but the brand does not.**

This is a deliberate architectural choice. The alternative — a sub-brand for hatcheries (e.g., "AquaWise Hatchery Pro") — would fragment the brand, multiply the marketing surface area, and dilute the trust capital we are building. Worse, it would suggest the hatchery product is somehow separate from the farmer product, when in fact they are two sides of the same closed loop.

Practical implications:

- **One name.** AquaWise. Not AquaWise Hatchery, not AquaWise Pro, not AquaWise for Hatcheries. The product *for* hatcheries; the brand is AquaWise.
- **One visual system.** Same Logo Blue, same fonts, same aesthetic across farmer surfaces and hatchery surfaces. A farmer who sees the AquaWise certificate and a hatchery owner who sees the AquaWise dashboard should feel the same brand.
- **One personality.** The ลูกหลาน persona governs every customer-facing surface, regardless of which side of the market the customer is on.
- **Stakeholder-specific messaging.** The benefits, the use cases, the testimonials vary by stakeholder. The brand foundation does not.

## 10. Brand-Building Roadmap

Brand equity is built over years, not quarters. The following is the roadmap for AquaWise hatchery brand development by phase. Each phase builds on the previous.

### Year 1 (2026): Salience + Performance

The goal is to be known and to work. The performance bar is high; the salience bar is low (just the beachhead community).

By December 2026:
- 100% of Chachoengsao Shrimp Larvae Hatchery Association members can describe AquaWise correctly in one sentence.
- 5 hatcheries actively using the product.
- Zero documented data accuracy issues.
- One published case study (P'Pong's hatchery, with consent).

### Year 2 (2027): Imagery + Judgments

The goal is for the brand to mean something, not just exist. The performance is now expected; we add layers of meaning and earn rational respect.

By December 2027:
- The "ลูกหลาน" persona is consistently recognized across all customer surfaces.
- 25+ hatcheries actively using the product across central Thailand.
- The "anti-eFishery" positioning is something hatcheries say back to us, unprompted.
- The first defended-against-unfair-blame case study is published and circulating in the industry.

### Year 3 (2028): Feelings + Early Resonance

The goal is for hatcheries to feel something specific about AquaWise, and for early loyalty to crystallize.

By December 2028:
- Hatcheries describe AquaWise with words like "secure," "respected," "professional" in unprompted conversations.
- Quarterly AquaWise hatchery roundtable becomes an established industry event.
- Public scorecard launches with 30+ verified hatcheries.
- The first hatchery describes themselves as "an AquaWise hatchery" in association settings.

## 12. The Brand DNA — One Page

If everything in this document collapsed into one page, it would be this:

**Category:** The closed-loop trust system for Thai shrimp aquaculture.

**Mission:** Help hatchery owners run cleaner, more confident, more respected operations by tracking every batch from sale to harvest with verifiable data.

**Customer:** The careful, modern, association-affiliated mid-sized hatchery owner. 40–60 years old. 15+ years in the industry. Confident in their craft. Wants their work to be seen.

**Personality:** ลูกหลานที่เรียนมา. Calm. Direct. Patient with people. Anti-eFishery. The educated younger relative who came back to help.

**Values:** Truth over comfort. Farmers free, always. Neutrality is the moat. Slow is right. The hatchery owner is the protagonist.

**Promise:** *"You will know what happened to your PL after the sale. You will be defended when blamed unfairly. Your craft will be recognized."*

**Three words:** Closed loop. Free for farmers. No hardware.

**Visual identity:** Logo Blue (#004AAD). Dark Cyan (#008B8B). Two-tier type system: Inter + Noto Sans Thai + JetBrains Mono on product surfaces; Plus Jakarta Sans + Inter + Noto Sans Thai on brand surfaces. Calm, careful, authoritative.

**The feeling we evoke:** Security. Self-respect.

**The feeling we never evoke:** Excitement. Urgency. Fear.

**The story we tell:** A Chula computer engineering student, two PhD scientists, one association president in Chachoengsao. One closed loop. One careful step at a time.

**The North Star:** When a Thai hatchery owner thinks of trust in their industry, they think of AquaWise. Not as a vendor. As infrastructure.

## 13. Closing — The Brand as Discipline

Brand work is sometimes treated as decoration. For AquaWise it is the opposite. The brand is the discipline that prevents us from making the eFishery mistake. The brand is the lens through which we evaluate every product decision, every hire, every fundraising decision, every customer conversation.

When in doubt, the question is: *"Does this strengthen or weaken the brand pyramid as defined in this document?"*

If a feature would compromise data accuracy, it weakens performance. If a marketing campaign would create excitement, it weakens feelings. If a partnership would compromise neutrality, it weakens imagery. Each of these is a brand decision masquerading as something else.

The team that internalizes this document does not need a marketing function. The brand becomes the operating system. That is the goal.
